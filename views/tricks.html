Tricks for Solo Living

  • Sticky Note on the Door: Place a sticky note on your front door listing essential items to check before leaving, such as keys, phone, and wallet.
  • Emergency Supplies Near Your Bed: Keep emergency medications and a water bottle within easy reach near your bed.
  • Phone Widgets: Add widgets to your phone's home screen for quick access to the weather forecast and a clock showing the time difference with your home country.
  • To-Do Lists: Use a to-do list or reminder app, or keep a sticky note on your desk to track daily tasks and important deadlines like assignments.
  • Phone Stand: A phone stand may seem unnecessary at first, but it’s incredibly helpful for hands-free use.
  • Rain Jacket: While an umbrella is essential, a rain jacket is more practical and can be more useful in certain situations.
  • Clothes Rack: Instead of scattering clothes on chairs or the floor, use a clothes rack to organize your clothes, hats, and bags. Choose a size that fits your room.
  • Foldable Basket: A foldable basket is space-saving and useful for washing socks and underwear or even for soaking your feet in warm water after a long day.
  • Humidifier: If you often wake up with a dry throat, consider getting a small humidifier to place near your bed.
  • Food Containers: You don’t need to buy new containers—reuse ones from food deliveries. Alternatively, buy a few (3-4 are enough) for storing leftovers or meal prepping.
  • Organizers: Use organizers to sort socks, underwear, nail clippers, and other small personal items.
  • Reusable Shopping Bags: Always carry reusable shopping bags when you go out. They’re practical, cost-effective, and eco-friendly.
